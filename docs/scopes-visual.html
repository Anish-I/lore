Vault — Scopes, visualized

Who is allowed to read which knowledge — and how the same question gives different people different answers.

A scope is a label on a note that says “who may read this.” Every person belongs to a set of scopes. When you ask a question, Vault searches only the scopes you’re in.

1 · A person stacks scopes (rings nest outward)

ENTERPRISE · acme-corp
CIRCLE · acme-deal
TEAM · eng-team
PRIVATE
A

Alice (A) sits in every ring she belongs to at once:

alice-private eng-team acme-deal acme-corp

private = just you · team = your squad · circle = a cross-cutting group that isn’t a formal team (e.g. the Acme deal pod spanning sales + eng + legal) · enterprise = the whole company.

You see a note if your scope set contains that note’s tag. Permissions and search are the same operation.

3 · Knowledge survives a departure — just re-tag the scope

Alice retires alice-private → admin re-tags the notes → eng-team the whole team can now query her knowledge

No copying, no migration. The “knowledge transfer when someone leaves / is on PTO” promise is just changing a label. Scopes are the knowledge-transfer system.
